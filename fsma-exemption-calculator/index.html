Fully Exempt (Not Covered) — 21 CFR § 112.4(a)

Partially Exempt (Qualified Exemption) — 21 CFR § 112.5

A farm qualifies for a Qualified Exemption if:
1. Average annual total food sales over the preceding 3 years is less than the inflation-adjusted threshold ($— for —).
2. Sales to "qualified end-users" (consumers anywhere, or restaurants/retail food establishments in the same state, on the same Indian reservation, or within 275 miles) exceeds sales to all other buyers combined.

Not Exempt (Fully Covered) — 21 CFR Part 112

A farm is fully covered by the Produce Safety Rule when it qualifies for neither exemption above. This applies when average annual produce sales exceed $— and either:
1. Average annual total food sales are $— or more, or
2. Sales to qualified end-users do not exceed sales to all other buyers (i.e., 50% or less of food sales are direct/local).

Covered farms must comply with all applicable Produce Safety Rule standards — agricultural water, biological soil amendments, sprouts, domesticated & wild animals, worker training & health/hygiene, and equipment, tools & buildings — and keep the required records. Operators should complete a PSA Grower Training course to understand their obligations.

Exemption Recordkeeping & Bookkeeping Guidelines

To verify compliance for an inspector using your farm records:
• Gross Produce Sales: All sales of raw, fresh agricultural produce (fruits, vegetables, herbs, sprouts, nuts). Do not include sales of non-produce products like livestock, poultry, dairy, grain, or hay.
• Total Food Sales: All sales of food products for human or animal consumption. This includes raw produce, processed food, meat, dairy, eggs, grain, and livestock feed (such as hay/silage). Subtract non-food items like timber, wool, ornamental plants, or flowers.
• Qualified End-User Sales: Sales of food products directly to the consumer (via CSA, farmers' markets, farmstands, online direct sales) or to local restaurants or retail food establishments located in the same state/territory or within 275 miles.

💡 Recordkeeping Requirement: Farms claiming a Qualified Exemption MUST establish and keep a written record reflecting that an annual eligibility review was performed (21 CFR § 112.7). Records must be readily available for FDA inspection (21 CFR § 112.166); records stored offsite must be retrievable within 24 hours of request (21 CFR § 112.162(a)). Fully exempt farms should also keep sales records to document their non-covered status.
